Annie Jean Easley

The Human Computer in Hidden Figures

Annie Jean Easley was an American computer scientist, mathematician, and rocket scientist. She worked for the Lewis Research Center of the National Aeronautics and Space Administration and its predecessor, the National Advisory Committee for Aeronautics

Timeline of Annie's Accomplishments:

  • 1933 - Annie J. Easley was born in Birmingham, Alabama
  • 1955 - Easley began her career as a “human computer,” doing computations for researchers. This involved analyzing problems and doing calculations by hand.
  • 1963 - Easley began working on nuclear-powered rocket systems including the Centaur high-energy booster rocket, which had its first successful launch in 1963
  • 1977 - Completed a mathematics degree from Cleveland State University

"When people have their biases and prejudices, yes, I am aware. My head is not in the sand. But my thing is, if I can't work with you, I will work around you. I was not about to be discouraged that I'd walk away. That may be a solution for some people, but it's not mine."

--Annie Jean Easley
